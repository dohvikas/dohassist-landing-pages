The Problem

The Red Flags Hiding in Your Electronic Journal

The average convenience store loses $14,000+ per year to employee theft. These are the transaction types your POS records every day — but no one is reviewing them.

Void Abuse

Employees ring up a sale, wait for the customer to leave, then void the transaction and pocket the cash. Happens dozens of times per week.

Sweethearting

Cashiers give free or discounted items to friends and family. Without video-to-POS matching, you'll never know it's happening.

Abort Transactions

Cashier starts a transaction, collects payment, then aborts before it's recorded. The POS shows nothing — but the cash is gone.

Penny Rings & PLU Abuse

Ringing items at $0.01 or using incorrect PLU codes to give away merchandise. Rate check abuse to test prices and manipulate transactions.

No-Sale & Till Tapping

Opening the drawer without a transaction. Small amounts removed throughout a shift — $20 here, $50 there — adds up to thousands per month.

Refund Fraud & Cash Shortages

Fake refunds processed with no actual return. Register is short every day but you can't prove who's responsible. Safe drop counts never match.
